Introduction

My name is Christian Everhart and I created this page to introduce myself. Resumes and career website profiles are carefully crafted to show your qualifications, but often fail to tell your personal story. Cover letters are also tricky, we do our best to convey passion about the role without sounding phony or too eager. Hopefully this site will convey what I'm truly passionate about and convince you that we could be a great team.

Career

I've always been curious about the technology we use to work with data. From a distance, my career has been all about data - many different formats, uses, and tools. If you look a little closer, the picture becomes more clear - my career has really been about the technologies.

Software

Pushing the correct buttons is important to get produce deliverables, but I always wonder what happens "under the hood" when I push that button. What about the rest of the buttons on the toolbars and menus? The answers to that question vary in complexity and usefulness, but satisfying the curiosity is always worth the effort.

Process

The processes used to create, modify, or combine data intrigue me. I've learned a lot from workflows and pipelines that are elegant and efficient, but also from the poorly designed ones. Building a new process is always fun, but improving an existing process is also very satisfying.

Storage

I've had the privilege to work with data stored in many different ways. Relational and unstructured, managed in folders or database systems, loaded into desktop applications or displayed in a browser - all of them have a place in our data landscape.

Education

Formal

I considered geography as a major when a geologist friend suggested that remote sensing might be interesting to me. That geologist was right and that was when my passion for data and technology started. Pictures of Earth from space are cool. Laying a paper map out on the table or floor and planning out a trip is fun. Looking at a map in a hallway is a great way to kill a few minutes. My resume shows you that my education is in geography, but there's so much more to the story.

Values

When I wrote out my list of values, one stood out among the others on the list - balance. Productivity brings balance both professionally and personally. Passion is important and necessary, but must be balanced with the discipline to execute. Projects with balanced collaboration and focus time give us pride in our work while building camaraderie. Balance is the value that governs the rest and something I strive for continuously.

Hobbies

Hobbies are considered unnecessary on our dry and boring resumes, but we still learn this from our colleagues after getting the job. I'm going to throw a couple out that do help me in my professional life - mountain biking and swimming. Those two sports may be completely different in many way, but both encourage progression. Whether you clean a challenging technical climb for the first time or set a PR for your 1K swim, you had to earn that mentally and physically over time. Those sports differ in big ways, also. With mountain biking, fun is the product which is earned by taking on the physical challenge. Swimming requires your full attention, making it a great way to clear your head and truly escape. Everything I mentioned above influences professional life in significant ways.
